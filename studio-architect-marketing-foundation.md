# Studio Architect — Marketing Foundation

## The Big Idea

**"The workflow no one told you about"**

*A complete photography workflow — from SD card to client delivery — that just works.*

---

## The Problem We're Solving

Beginning and hobbyist photographers face a frustrating reality: no one tells them how to actually run the business side of photography. Established photographers guard their workflows like trade secrets. YouTube tutorials cover bits and pieces, but never the whole picture.

New photographers waste months:
- Cobbling together Lightroom + Pixieset + Dropbox + spreadsheets
- Asking "what's your workflow?" in Facebook groups and getting vague answers
- Figuring out the hard way how to organize, cull, edit, deliver, and archive thousands of images

**Studio Architect is the guide they've been looking for** — a complete workflow, opinionated defaults, and software that just works so they can focus on what they love: taking pictures.

Priced separately from the session model because it carries genuine recurring storage/bandwidth cost. **Opt-in only:** it does not change the local-first, "your files stay on your machine" default — nothing uploads unless the photographer chooses it. *(Delivered via a third-party gallery platform first; our own hosting only if demand is proven.)*

A side-hustle photographer charging $300/session pays $3.50 (one session from a 10-pack) — that's about 1% of revenue for the entire workflow. Easy ROI.

## Brand Structure

### Company
**Apps By Adam** — Developer name, subtle presence. Shows up in App Store, not in marketing.

### Products

| Product | Role | Pricing |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Studio Architect** | Main brand, complete native workflow app | Session-based (see above) |
| CullSnap | Standalone culling app — **transitional** | $2-5 one-time |
| FirstPass | Standalone basic edits app — **transitional** | $2-5 one-time |
| TriPod | Standalone import/backup app — **transitional** | $2-5 one-time |

The standalone apps are **separate products with no link to Studio Architect**. They serve two roles during the build-out: (1) low-cost acquisition funnel — a beginner buys a cheap single-purpose app, hits its ceiling, and graduates to Studio Architect; (2) feature incubators where ideas are validated before being **rebuilt natively** in Studio Architect. They are **sunset over time** as Studio Architect matures.

### Inside Studio Architect
Studio Architect implements every stage **natively** (it does not launch or embed the standalone apps):
1. Import — native SD-card ingest
2. Cull — native ML-assisted culling
3. Basic Edits — native auto-adjustments → XMP
4. Detail Edits — handoff to the user's external editor (Lightroom/Capture One)
5. Export
6. Deliver
7. Archive

## Launch Strategy

### Phase 1: Soft Launch (Now)
**Goal:** Validate individual apps, gather feedback, build early audience

- Ship CullSnap, FirstPass, TriPod to App Store
- Price: $2-5 each
- Minimal marketing — organic discovery
- Collect feedback, fix bugs, stabilize
- Learn what resonates

### Phase 2: Waitlist
**Goal:** Build anticipation for Studio Architect

- Launch landing page with tagline and value prop
- Email capture: "Be the first to know"
- Share origin story (your daughter's frustration finding workflow info)
- Light content marketing — YouTube, Reddit, Instagram

### Phase 3: Build
**Goal:** Develop the complete native Studio Architect app

- Build the native workflow stages and dashboard inside one app
- Pull validated features from the incubator apps (CullSnap/FirstPass/TriPod) and rebuild them natively
- Beta test with waitlist members
- Iterate based on feedback

### Phase 4: Launch
**Goal:** Public release of Studio Architect

- Early access to waitlist (reward them for waiting)
- Launch pricing / early bird lifetime deals
- Ramp up content marketing
- Collect testimonials and case studies
